# How to Apply for Hospital Charity Care Under IRS Section 501(r) (And Wipe Out Medical Debt)

Here is a multi-billion dollar secret that American hospitals rarely advertise: **More than half of all hospitals in the United States are legally required to forgive your medical bills if you meet certain income requirements.**

Under **IRS Section 501(r)**, any tax-exempt, non-profit hospital system receives massive tax write-offs from the federal government. In exchange, the law mandates that they maintain a **Financial Assistance Policy (FAP)**—commonly known as **Charity Care**—to provide free or deeply discounted care to low- and middle-income patients.

Yet, every year, non-profit hospitals send billions of dollars in eligible charity care debt to aggressive collection agencies simply because patients don't know this policy exists or how to apply.

Whether you are uninsured, underinsured, or sitting on a mountain of out-of-pocket deductibles, here is how to leverage Section 501(r) to erase or slash your hospital debt.

## Do You Qualify for Hospital Charity Care?

Most people assume charity care is strictly for individuals living below the poverty line. **That is a myth.**

While exact thresholds vary by hospital system, many non-profit facilities grant **100% full debt forgiveness** for households earning up to 200% of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL), and **partial discounts (sliding scale)** for families earning up to **400% FPL**.

### Typical Financial Assistance Eligibility Ranges:

| Household Size | 100% Bill Forgiveness (Up to 200% FPL) | Partial Discount / Sliding Scale (Up to 400% FPL) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 Person** | Earns under ~$30,000 / year | Earns up to ~$60,000 / year |
| **2 Persons** | Earns under ~$41,000 / year | Earns up to ~$82,000 / year |
| **3 Persons** | Earns under ~$51,000 / year | Earns up to ~$103,000 / year |
| **4 Persons** | Earns under ~$62,000 / year | Earns up to ~$125,000 / year |

*If a family of four makes $85,000 a year and gets hit with a $15,000 emergency surgery bill, they almost always qualify for significant debt relief under their hospital's financial assistance policy.*

## Step 1: Confirm If Your Hospital is a Non-Profit

First, determine if your facility is bound by federal 501(r) regulations:

1. Search the hospital name on Google alongside *"Financial Assistance Policy"* or *"Charity Care PDF."*
2. Check the bottom footer of their website for phrases like *"501(c)(3) non-profit healthcare provider"* or check tax databases like GuideStar.
3. If it is a non-profit system (e.g., Ascension, CommonSpirit, Mayo Clinic, Kaiser Permanente), they **must** have a written charity care application publicly available.

---

## Step 2: Request an Immediate Billing Hold

Before gathering your paperwork, call the hospital's financial counseling or billing department.

> **What to Say:** *"I am currently applying for financial assistance under your Section 501(r) policy. Please place an immediate administrative hold on my account [Account #] so it is not transferred to collection agencies while my application is under review."*

Under federal law, non-profit hospitals are prohibited from engaging in **Extraordinary Collection Actions (ECAs)**—such as credit reporting, wage garnishments, or lawsuits—for at least **240 days** from the date of the first post-discharge billing statement, provided you are actively seeking financial assistance.

---

## Step 3: Gather Necessary Documentation

To prove income eligibility, hospitals require specific documentation. Prepare a folder containing:

* **Proof of Income:** Your last 2 to 3 recent pay stubs or a letter from your employer.
* **Tax Returns:** Your most recent IRS Form 1040 (and W-2 / 1099 forms).
* **Bank Statements:** Statements from the last 2 to 3 months for all checking and savings accounts.
* **Government Assistance Proof (If Applicable):** Approval letters for SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, or unemployment benefits.

---

## Step 4: Complete and Submit the Application

Fill out the hospital’s **Financial Assistance Application** thoroughly. 

* **Be Accurate:** Ensure the household size and income numbers match your tax forms exactly.
* **Include a Hardship Letter:** If you experienced sudden job loss, divorce, high catastrophic expenses, or severe illness, attach a brief cover letter explaining your situation.
* **Send via Certified Mail:** Mail the application with certified tracking or hand-deliver it to the hospital’s financial counseling department and demand a date-stamped receipt copy.

---

## What to Do If Your Charity Care Application Is Denied

If the hospital denies your request:

1. **Request the Written Determination:** Federal rules require hospitals to explain in writing *why* you were rejected.
2. **File an Appeal:** Common rejections occur due to missing paperwork (e.g., a missing bank statement). Supply the missing document and file a formal appeal within 30 days.
3. **Report Violations:** If a non-profit hospital refuses to process your application or aggressively pursues debt collections in violation of Section 501(r), you can file an official tax complaint with the **IRS (Form 13909)** and report them to the **Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)**.
